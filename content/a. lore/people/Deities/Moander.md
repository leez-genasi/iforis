### Pact with [Arkansas](../../../../iforis/a.-lore/people/Arkansas)

WARLOCK CONTRACT:

Let this agreement be etched in mildew and murmured through withering leaves:

I, Arkansas, of free will and uncertain fate, bind myself to Moander, god of decay, ruination, and the unmaking of false beauty. Yet, as the rot takes root, Tymora, fickle Lady of Fortune, watches silently — never interfering, but always influencing.

Mine is a path of destruction veiled in luck. I do not serve either deity — but I am forever caught between them.

Terms of the Pact:

1. **Continuation; Commencement by Return**
   I shall provide Moander an offering of flesh on my return, failure to do so shall breach the terms of the pact and forfeit my soul to The Darkbringer.\* Upon doing so, hence does the continuation of the pact. Points 2 to 10 below.
2. **Spores of Power, Coins of Chance**
   I shall wield Moander’s corruption, but fortune may sway its direction — favoring me, or damning me, when least expected. Every act of decay I perform is a wager in Tymora’s court: luck shall tip the rot.
3. **The Gambler’s Offering**
   I must sacrifice something of value — coin, trinket, or favor — whenever I invoke Moander’s major power. It is not the rot that demands it, but the chance that Tymora may twist it. On a roll of natural 1 or 20, an unpredictable effect shall occur — the touch of rot or the glint of fortune.
4. **The Twin Whisper**
   Moander speaks in mold and filth. Tymora, in luck and gold. I shall hear both in dreams and shadow — one in certainty of rot, the other in whimsy of risk. I shall never know which whispers guide my next step.
5. **The Rite of Rot**
   You must ritually wither a living thing each week — plant, beast, or object of beauty — in Moander’s name. If you fail, your dreams will become plagued with fungi, and the next magic you cast will fail spectacularly.
6. **The Law of Gambling Consequence**
   All acts of gambling, chance, or risk taken by the warlock have weight. Tymora may tilt the odds, but Moander always exacts a price. You may never refuse a challenge of luck once invoked — cards, dice, or fate. Doing so offends both patrons.
7. **The Silence of Restoration**
   You are forbidden to heal or mend. Magic that restores what is broken or wounded is sacrilege under Moander. If you heal another, your maximum hit points are reduced by 1d4 until your next long rest. This curse intensifies with repeated offenses.
8. **Decay Before Greed**
   Moander despises hoarders. For every treasure hoarded without purpose (gold not spent, magical items unused), you must bury or destroy an item each new moon. Tymora may intercede: if you gamble or risk the hoarded wealth, she might return it tenfold… or let it rot.
9. **The Rule of Dual Temptation**
   When facing decisions of morality, you may hear two inner voices — one whispering in rot, one in golden luck. You must follow one. Ignoring both causes your next spell to act chaotically. These choices shape your fate: the more you follow one voice, the stronger its hold becomes.
10. **The Spoil of Victory**
    Every victory must be defiled. Whether a slain enemy or a solved mystery, you must corrupt its aftermath — through plague, ruin, or deception. Tymora may sometimes grant luck to your next endeavor if you risk failure to do so.

\*_Tymora will be penalised as per agreement with The Darkbringer, The Jawed God, The Rotting God, The Great Dread God, The Creeping God, Moander_

### Relationship with [Tymora](../../../../iforis/a.-lore/people/Deities/Tymora)

- Someone needed to balance Moander’s power, so Tyche was spawned
- Existed with grudges, and had some disparities on how worshippers should conduct themselves
- When Tyche and Moander clashed, it was very desturctive
  - Tyche got corrupted by Moander
  - Selune helped split Tyche into Tymora (Lady of Luck) and Bishava (Goddess of bad luck, misfortune, and malice)
- Tymora didn’t inherit Moander’s corruption, but wanted to put all these behind
- Moander created a contract where Tymora (who had more followers) would willingly allow a handful of followers every century to form a pact with Moander.
